Meet the inventor of Instagram: Kevin Systrom

Instagram's founder Kevin Systrom
  • Born December 30, 1983 in Holliston, Massachusetts
  • Attended Stanford University Science and Engineering
  • Spend winter term of Junior year in Florence studying photography
  • One of twelve students selected for the Mayfield Fellows Program at Stanford University
  • Internship at Odea, the company that eventually gave rise to Twitter
  • Graduated from Stanford University in 2006 with B.A. Management
  • Hired as an Associate Product Marketing Manager working on Gmail, Google Calendar, Docs, Spreadsheets and other products
  • Changed companies to work for Nextstop in 2010
  • While on vacation, he got the idea to create the X-Pro II filter to improve his girlfriends picture she took on her iPhone.
  • Pitched his idea at a party to Baseline Ventures and Andreessen Horowitz.
  • Quit his job to explore starting a photo filtering company called Burbn.
  • Received $500,000 seed funding from Baseline Ventures and Andreessen Horowitz to launch Burbn
  • Refined Burbn into the photo-sharing app Instagram (Instant Telegram)
  • One month after launching, Instagram grows to 1 million users.
  • One year later, Instagram hit more than 10 million users
  • In 2015, System was listed in the Forbes 30 user 30 list
  • As of October 2015, over 40 billion pictures had been shared on Instagram
  • As of June 2016, Instagram has over 500 million active users
  • Plans to get involved in Virtual Reality products
